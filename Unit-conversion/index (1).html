What is a milligram?

A milligram is a metric unit of mass equal to one-thousandth of a gram. It is commonly used for medicines, supplements, lab measurements, and very small quantities of material.

Because it represents a tiny amount, milligrams are useful when precision matters and grams would be too large a unit.

What is a gram?

A gram is a metric unit of mass widely used in everyday life and science. It is common for food labels, ingredients, small packages, and laboratory measurements.

Since 1000 milligrams make 1 gram, converting between the two is straightforward and ideal for a simple calculator like this one.

How to convert mg to g

Divide the number of milligrams by 1000. For example: 15 mg ÷ 1000 = 0.015 g.

This works because the gram is a larger unit, so the number becomes smaller when converting upward.
